Not a productivity app

Other tools optimize your day.
You're trying to keep a decade in view.

Habit and task apps

Streaks, points, and “get things done” for whatever you type in. They don't know your test window, your job-search season, or why this launch week is different from last. Trov is rooted in your timeline and real gates, not a generic list.

Calendars and blockers

They protect time. They don't tell you the order of work inside those blocks across two years, or which requirement moves when you slip a deadline. Trov links your clock to a sequence, not an empty block.

Notes, docs, and “workspaces”

A beautiful place to write is still a blank page. Trov is not where you store ideas — it is the voice that asks, each week, whether you're on pace for the gate that actually decides your life.

Why the long game is brutal

You know where you're headed.
You don't have a map.

A thousand unwritten rules

The promotion, the license, the program, the shipped product — the finish line is clear. The order of what to do first, what to cut, and what to ignore is not. You're piecing the path together from friends, forums, and fear.

Time isn't the problem. Structure is.

Day job, side project, family, the exam, the app you're rewriting — every week competes. Real deadlines and seasons don't wait while you “figure it out later.”

Fifteen minutes, once a quarter

Advisors, managers, and mentors are swamped. The stakes are years of your life — and the personal guidance doesn't scale to everyone who needs it.

The prep deluge

Ten prep companies, a hundred Anki decks, and hot takes for every test date. You don't need more noise — you need the right work, for your exam and your exact timeline.
